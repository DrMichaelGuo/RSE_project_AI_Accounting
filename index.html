The Impact of AI on the Future of Accounting in the UK

An Investigation into Evolving Roles and Ethical Challenges

Principal Investigator

Dr Michael Guo

University of the West of Scotland, UK

Research Summary

This research aims to examine the impact of Artificial Intelligence (AI) on the UK's accounting sector. Employing a mixed-methods approach, the research addresses a knowledge gap in empirical studies by investigating the first-hand experiences of accounting professionals navigating this technological shift.

Operational Efficiency

Investigating how AI technologies are enhancing productivity and streamlining accounting processes in UK firms.

Skill Set Evolution

Examining the changing competencies required for accounting professionals in an AI-augmented workplace.

Professional Adaptability

Analysing how accounting professionals are adapting to technological changes and evolving their roles.

Ethical Challenges

Exploring issues such as data privacy, algorithmic fairness, and the potential for job displacement in the accounting sector.

Emerging Insights

Implications for Accounting Professionals

Shift in Work Nature

A fundamental shift from manual and repetitive tasks towards more strategic and analytical roles.

Continuous Upskilling

The critical need for continuous upskilling and development of new competencies, encompassing both technical and "soft" skills.

Ethical Responsibility

Increased responsibility for ethical considerations and risk management associated with AI adoption.

Advice for Accounting Students

Develop Digital Skills

Learn how to use AI and analytics in accounting tasks; it's key for future roles.

Adapt to New Roles

Focus on analysis, decision-making, and sustainability as routine tasks become automated.

Continuous Learning

Stay curious, build professional judgement, and keep learning AI and ESG-related skills.

Strengthen Communication

Clear reporting and communication are essential alongside technical knowledge.
